Research Project Website

Storypal makes storybooks more accessible, expressive, and inclusive.

Storypal is a web-based research project focused on helping children with visual, hearing, and speech impairments experience storybooks through text extraction, emotional narration, sign language support, and speech assistance.

Explore the Research View Project Files

Abstract

This project combines OCR, emotion recognition, emotional text-to-speech, sign language translation, and stuttering detection into one multimodal platform that reduces access barriers in conventional children's storybooks.

Inclusive Focus

Visual, hearing, and speech accessibility in one platform.

Highlights

  • Emotion-aware storytelling
  • ASL phrase translation
  • Speech therapy assistance

Domain

Technical and academic foundation

The Storypal research domain combines accessibility design with machine learning, language processing, and speech technologies to address a real educational gap.

Literature Survey

Existing work in OCR, image captioning, CNN-LSTM pipelines, and assistive reading tools provides a strong baseline, but few solutions address children with multiple impairments in a unified storytelling experience.

Research Gap

Drop cap letter detection in storybooks remains limited, and there is still a lack of integrated multimodal systems that combine audio narration, sign translation, and speech therapy features in one workflow.

Research Problem

Conventional storybooks are difficult to access for children with disabilities when text is embedded in images, narration lacks emotional context, sign-based support is missing, and speech-related interaction is not considered.

Research Objectives

  • Extract text from illustrated storybooks
  • Recognize emotional tone using BERT
  • Generate emotional narration with Coqui XTTS
  • Translate content into sign language support
  • Detect stuttering patterns for speech assistance

Methodology

Storypal integrates CRAFT for text region detection, BERT for emotion classification, emotional speech synthesis pipelines, and Random Forest-based speech analysis to build a complete assistive storytelling pipeline.

Technologies Used

MERN stack components, Next.js, Python, VGG-16, Detectron2, and complementary AI models are used to support the application interface, data handling, and model inference layers.

Milestones

Project timeline

The major phases are grouped into expandable milestones so visitors can scan the research journey without reading everything at once.

Project Proposal Initial concept and sub-objective definition

The proposal phase established the Storypal concept, framed the problem space, and divided the project into focused research objectives for implementation.

Progress Presentation 1 OCR and emotion classification development

This stage covered early system progress, especially text extraction from storybooks and emotion classification using BERT-based language processing models.

Progress Presentation 2 TTS, sign language, and stuttering modules

The second progress review focused on emotional text-to-speech generation, sign language translation support, and the implementation of the stuttering detection pipeline.

Final Assessment / Viva Validation, benchmarking, and pilot testing

The final evaluation assessed overall system performance against benchmarks and pilot test outcomes to validate Storypal as an inclusive storytelling solution.
